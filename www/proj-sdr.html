Array Signal Dimension Reduction

High-dimensional array signals can be represented in a low-dimensional subspace without Fisher information loss

Abstract

Large-scale array systems create opportunities of providing centimeter-level location awareness. Despite their promise, the communication overhead and the computational complexities grow linearly and cubically as the number of antennas increases, which will occupy large amounts of time-frequency resources, incur long latency, and fail to meet real-time requirements. As a solution, we establish a general framework of array signal dimension reduction for network localization and sensing, which sheds light on high-accuracy localization with limited resource consumption.

Highlight

  • Prove that high-dimensional array signals can be represented in a low-dimensional subspace without Fisher information loss.

  • Present the closed-form expressions of such lossless array dimension reduction methods.
